Overall Strategy

The full strategic narrative for the LGR Enterprise Architecture Operating Model.

Executive Summary

The Enterprise Architecture Operating Model provides Stevenage Borough Council with a practical, supportive and structured way to manage Local Government Reorganisation. It uses four architecture domains, five service clusters and a distinct AI governance route to reduce risk, create clarity and support better resident outcomes.

Four architects

Business, Data, Application & Digital, and Technology & Security domain leadership.

Five clusters

Customer, People, Place, Corporate and Technology foundations.

Separate AI model

AI governed as a strategic cross-cutting capability.

TOGAF tailored

TOGAF ADM used pragmatically for LGR pace and complexity.

Strategic Purpose

The purpose of the strategy is to establish an architecture function that is supportive, practical and able to guide LGR decisions without overwhelming individual architects, service teams or governance boards.

  • Create a clear operating model for the four Enterprise Architects.
  • Organise LGR work into logical service clusters.
  • Provide SLT with clear choices, risks, dependencies and trade-offs.
  • Support vesting day readiness and post-vesting optimisation.
  • Ensure county, district and borough services are brought together coherently.
  • Make AI visible, governed and aligned to public-sector values.
Strategic message: four architects, five service clusters, one shared architecture method and one distinct AI governance route.
