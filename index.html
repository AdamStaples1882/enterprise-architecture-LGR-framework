Why this model exists

The model exists to manage Local Government Reorganisation in a structured, supportive and low-risk way. It prevents one architect from carrying the full burden, gives SLT a clear view of risk and dependencies, and helps services fit together logically from the resident front door through to shared digital foundations.

Supportive by design

Work is distributed across four architects, with shared governance and visible prioritisation.

Resident-led

The sequence starts with Customer Services and follows the resident journey into operational and enabling services.

Clear accountability

Each architect has a defined domain, linked to service areas, outputs and evidence.

Better decisions

SLT gets clearer options, risks, dependencies, trade-offs and escalation routes.

Executive message: this is not a technical structure for its own sake. It is a practical delivery model for safer LGR design and transition.

Validation caveat

Important: the service and capability coverage in this portal has been checked against national county/district responsibilities and public Hertfordshire and Stevenage service categories. It is a strong architecture baseline, but it must be validated through formal LGR discovery with service owners, statutory officers, finance, HR, legal, information governance, suppliers and programme governance before it is used as a final transition inventory.

Architect focus

The four architects operate as a single supportive team. Each has a primary domain, but the major LGR decisions are made through joined-up review.

Business Architect

Operating model, service design, capability mapping, value streams, customer journeys and business change.

Data Architect

Data domains, quality, migration, information governance, reporting, insight and data sharing.

Application and Digital Architect

Applications, CRM, ERP, portals, case systems, integrations, supplier dependencies and rationalisation.

Technology and Security Architect

Cloud, identity, networks, devices, cyber controls, resilience, disaster recovery and technical standards.

AI operating model: separate for a reason

AI is not just another technology tool. It is a cross-cutting strategic capability that needs visible governance, ethical controls, human oversight and ongoing monitoring.

Why AI is separate

  • It affects all services and architecture domains.
  • It creates opportunity and public trust risk.
  • It can introduce bias, privacy and security concerns.
  • It needs visible SLT assurance.

How it works

  • Identify the problem and expected value.
  • Assess business, data, application and technology impact.
  • Govern through the AI Architecture Board.
  • Pilot safely with human oversight.
  • Monitor benefits, fairness, drift and compliance.

Overall strategy

The Enterprise Architecture Operating Model gives Stevenage a practical, supportive and structured way to manage Local Government Reorganisation. It uses four architecture domains, five service clusters and a distinct AI governance route to reduce risk, create clarity and support better resident outcomes.

Four architects

Business, Data, Application and Digital, and Technology and Security domain leadership.

Five clusters

Customer, People, Place, Corporate and Technology foundations.

Separate AI model

AI governed as a strategic cross-cutting capability.

TOGAF tailored

TOGAF ADM used pragmatically for LGR pace and complexity.

Strategic objectives

ObjectiveDescriptionExpected value
1. Establish a supportive four-architect modelDefine clear domain leadership across Business, Data, Application and Digital, and Technology and Security.Reduced overload, clearer accountability and better decisions.
2. Organise LGR through service clustersUse five clusters to structure discovery, design and transition planning.Better sequencing from resident front door to enabling foundations.
3. Align applications and dataMap services to systems, data, integrations and transition dependencies.Reduced duplication and clearer readiness planning.
4. Govern AI separatelyUse AI Architecture Board and risk-tiered approval.Responsible innovation with public trust and assurance.
