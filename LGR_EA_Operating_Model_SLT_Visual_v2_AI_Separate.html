LGR Enterprise Architecture Operating Model

Simple view for SLT: why the model exists, how work is organised, what each architect focuses on, and why AI needs a distinct operating model

Purpose of the model

This operating model is designed to help Stevenage manage Local Government Reorganisation in a structured, supportive and low-risk way. It uses four architects with clear domain leadership, but shared responsibility, so no single architect carries the full burden of LGR design and assurance.

Supportive by design Spreads workload and reduces dependency on one individual.
Resident-led Starts with Customer Services and works logically through operational and enabling services.
Clear accountability Each architect has a primary focus, while major decisions are reviewed together.
Better LGR decisions Improves pace, reduces risk and gives SLT clearer choices and trade-offs.

How the work is organised

1

Customer, Access and Resident Experience

The front door: contact, website, CRM, access, triage and resident communication.

2

Community, Housing and People Services

Housing, homelessness, community safety, adult social care, children’s services, SEND and public health.

3

Place, Environment and Regulatory Services

Planning, waste, highways, transport, environmental health, licensing, trading standards and resilience.

4

Corporate and Enabling Services

HR, Finance, Payroll, Legal, Procurement, Democratic Services and wider corporate capability.

5

Technology and Shared Platforms

Core technology, integrations, identity, cyber, reporting and shared digital foundations.

The service clusters give the team a logical order for discovery, design and transition planning, rather than treating LGR as a collection of disconnected projects.

Architect focus and rationale

Business Architect

Primary focus: operating model, service design and business change.

  • Leads target operating model design.
  • Maps capabilities, processes and customer journeys.
  • Connects resident need to service outcomes.
  • Shapes service-cluster design and transition impacts.
Rationale: this role keeps architecture grounded in service reality, so LGR decisions improve how the council works rather than simply moving systems around.

Data Architect

Primary focus: data consistency, quality and insight.

  • Defines core data domains such as resident, property, case, asset, employee and finance.
  • Leads data quality, migration and reporting design.
  • Supports information governance and data-sharing decisions.
  • Improves the evidence base for LGR decision-making.
Rationale: this role helps prevent fragmented records, poor reporting and migration risk, which are common failure points in reorganisation.

Application & Digital Architect

Primary focus: applications, digital services and integration.

  • Maps the application estate across the service clusters.
  • Leads CRM, ERP, portal and case-system alignment.
  • Identifies consolidation, integration and supplier dependencies.
  • Supports rationalisation and future digital service design.
Rationale: this role ensures the future authority is not left with duplicated systems, unclear interfaces or disconnected digital services.

Technology & Security Architect

Primary focus: infrastructure, cyber, identity and resilience.

  • Defines the shared technology foundation.
  • Leads cloud, network, device and identity design.
  • Shapes cyber security and resilience controls.
  • Supports safe vesting day access and service continuity.
Rationale: this role reduces operational and cyber risk, and ensures the new authority can function safely from day one.

Why AI needs to be separate

Why set up a distinct AI operating model

  • AI is cross-cutting: it can affect Customer Services, casework, reporting, HR, Finance and operational delivery at the same time.
  • AI creates both opportunity and risk: it can improve productivity and insight, but can also create bias, poor decisions, security issues and resident trust concerns.
  • AI needs visible governance: SLT needs assurance that AI use is lawful, ethical, secure and proportionate.
  • AI should not enter the organisation informally: a defined model prevents uncontrolled use of tools without oversight.

How the AI operating model works

  • All AI ideas start with a clear business problem and expected outcome.
  • The four architects review the idea across business, data, application and technology impacts.
  • An AI Architecture Board decides whether the proposal should proceed, proceed with conditions or stop.
  • Approved use cases are piloted, monitored and reviewed before wider rollout.
  • Ongoing monitoring checks value, fairness, security, compliance and resident impact.
1. Identify Define the service problem, opportunity and expected value.
2. Assess Review business, data, application, technology, security and equality implications.
3. Govern AI Architecture Board approves, rejects or applies conditions.
4. Pilot Test safely, with human oversight and clear success measures.
5. Monitor Track benefits, bias, compliance, security and user impact.
For SLT, the key point is simple: AI should be treated as a strategic capability with formal governance, not just as another technology tool.

What SLT should take from this

One team, four leads Clear domain ownership without isolated silos.
Logical service order Resident-facing services first, enabling services after, shared foundations underneath.
Separate AI governance AI is important enough to need its own visible controls and approval route.
Lower delivery risk Shared review of major LGR decisions improves quality, pace and vesting day readiness.
