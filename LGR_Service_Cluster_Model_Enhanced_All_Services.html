Enhanced LGR Service Cluster Model: Full Service Coverage

District, borough and county services organised into one resident-led future unitary architecture model

Purpose

This enhanced model expands the original Appendix B service cluster view so it captures the wider set of local authority services that may come together through Local Government Reorganisation.

It keeps the five-cluster structure but adds fuller county, district, borough and cross-cutting services so discovery, application mapping, data mapping and transition planning can be more complete.

Design rule

Services are placed in their main architectural home. This is not a rigid organisational boundary: many services, such as SEND, public health, emergency planning, finance and GIS, will span more than one cluster.

Service flow: resident need → people and place services → corporate enablement → shared digital, data and technology foundations

Coverage check by local government responsibility

County-scale responsibilities
Education, transport, strategic planning, fire and public safety, social care, libraries, waste management and trading standards are represented across clusters 1, 2, 3 and 5.
District / borough responsibilities
Housing, local planning, waste collection, council tax and revenues, environmental health, leisure, local land, licensing, parking and customer access are represented across clusters 1, 2, 3 and 4.
Unitary authority outcome
The model brings county and district responsibilities into one architecture view so the future authority can plan service continuity, transition states, data dependencies and shared platforms coherently.
Thinking and justification: the enhanced model uses the LGA and GOV.UK distinction between county, district and unitary responsibilities as a baseline, then groups the services by resident journey and operating model rather than by legacy organisational ownership. This gives Stevenage a practical LGR architecture model that is comprehensive, service-aware and manageable for a four-architect team.

Source basis used for service coverage:

Local Government Association: local government organisation and LGR resources, including the explanation that many areas have two tiers with responsibility split between county and district councils, while unitary authorities are responsible for all local services.

GOV.UK: “Understand how your council works: Types of council”, which summarises county responsibilities such as education, transport, planning, fire and public safety, social care, libraries, waste management and trading standards, and district responsibilities such as rubbish collection, recycling, Council Tax, housing and planning applications.
